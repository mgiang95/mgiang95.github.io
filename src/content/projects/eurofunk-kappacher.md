## Problem

Emergency response software fails in ways office software never does: users
operate it under time pressure, in vehicles, with gloves, while lives depend
on the outcome. Requirements gathered at a desk do not survive contact with
that reality.

## Approach

I conducted contextual user research with the Salzburg Fire Brigade and the
Austrian Red Cross — observing real operations rather than interviewing
about them — and translated the findings into user requirements and an
interactive prototype for in-vehicle response coordination.

## Outcome

Key features from the prototype were later implemented in the live
[resQnect DRIVE system](https://www.eurofunk.com/products/command-control/resqnect-mobile-apps/)
— research that ended up in production, not in a report drawer.
