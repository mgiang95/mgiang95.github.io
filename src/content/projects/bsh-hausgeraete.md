## Problem

Oven control panels compress many functions into a small surface — and the
people using them have wet hands, hot trays and little patience for menu
diving. The task: rethink the interaction, not just the interface.

## Approach

I mapped the cooking flow as a user journey to find where controls actually
matter, then translated the concept into a physical prototype of an oven
control panel — including an NFC chip so settings could travel to the device
instead of being dialed in by hand.

## Outcome

The journey-first framing moved the discussion from "which buttons" to
"which moments", and the tangible prototype made the concept testable with
real gestures — groundwork for my later focus on interaction design beyond
the screen.
