## Problem

Tourists explore new places staring at their phones — the screen that is
supposed to help them experience a city is exactly what keeps them from
seeing it.

## Approach

Within the 24 hours of the GastroHackathon, our team designed and built
Wayve: a screenless wearable that guides through haptic and ambient cues
instead of a display. Points of interest and interactions with locals are
surfaced by the device — eyes stay on the city, not on a map.

## Outcome

A working prototype in one day, later featured by
[FH Salzburg](https://www.fh-salzburg.ac.at/en/study/ct/human-computer-interaction-joint-master/projects/wayve-a-wearable-device-for-tourists)
— and a compact proof that interaction design does not need a screen to be
useful.
