I started in web development in 2014, but my curiosity of the "why" behind
products led me to transition into design in 2018.

Since then, I've focused on creating and managing design systems, which are
sets of guidelines that help make products look and work consistently. I've
also become interested in UX governance and web accessibility, which is about
setting rules to ensure a good, unified user experience across all products.
Essentially, I make sure that the user experience is well-planned and aligns
with the needs of everyone involved.

I've been lucky to learn from amazing colleagues and from experts like Alice
Packard, Diana Mounter, and Nathan Curtis. Outside of work, I enjoy connecting
with others in Zurich at design events like Last Thursday Talks or Friends of
Figma.

Thanks to the broad nature of UX, I've been able to apply my design system
work to both consumer-focused (B2C) and business-focused (B2B) products, which
you can see in the following sections of my portfolio.
